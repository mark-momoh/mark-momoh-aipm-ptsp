## Problem & Motivation

There is no digital path for assigning POS terminals to merchants within the Aptpay
PTSP platform. Back office admins and third-party admins (acquirers) currently have
no portal or system through which to allocate terminals from inventory — meaning
terminal provisioning is entirely untracked, error-prone, and dependent on manual
coordination outside the platform.

---

## Current Behaviour

- The terminal provisioning flow does not exist on the Aptpay PTSP platform
- Terminal assignment from inventory to merchant is performed outside the system
- There is no tracking of terminal-merchant mappings or assignment status
- Duplicate terminal IDs and incorrect merchant mappings go undetected

---

## Desired Behaviour

- Back office admins can assign POS terminals to third-party admins via the portal
- Third-party admins can assign terminals to their merchants via the portal
- Assignments are validated to prevent duplicate terminal IDs and incorrect merchant
  mappings before a terminal goes live
- All terminal assignment activity is tracked with status visibility

---

## Channels

- `POS` — Terminal assignment and merchant-mapping data is committed to the terminal
  record and readable by the POS device once provisioned
- `INTERNAL_SYSTEM` — The back office and third-party admin portals through which
  all assignment actions are performed

## Scope & Considerations

**Out of scope:**
- Physical key injection
- SIM and connectivity setup
- Terminal repairs and maintenance
- Terminal decommissioning
- Agent onboarding

**Must not regress:** Existing terminal-merchant mappings in TMS — this release adds
new mappings only and must not modify or overwrite existing ones.

**Secondary impacts to monitor:** Transaction routing (CAPABILITY-TAPT-TRXN) depends
on correct terminal-merchant mappings established here — assignment errors in this
release will have downstream effects on transaction routing.
